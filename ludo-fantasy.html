Ludo Fantasy: Improving Early Funnel Conversion & Player Onboarding

A case study on analyzing and improving the early-player journey for a real-money casual game, focused on reducing onboarding friction and increasing first-session conversion.

Contextual Intro

During my time supporting the delivery team on Ludo Fantasy, I stepped into a hybrid BA role—mapping user journeys, reviewing early telemetry patterns, and helping clarify product requirements around onboarding and match entry. Although the data here is anonymized and simplified, the problems and decision-making reflect how I contributed to improving the early funnel experience.

Role & Responsibilities

  • Reviewed onboarding flow and identified friction points affecting first-session entry.
  • Translated usability issues into structured requirements for PM and engineering.
  • Worked with QA to validate event triggers and onboarding consistency.
  • Supported definition of early funnel KPIs with analytics teams.
  • Collaborated with design on simplifying the first-play experience.

Problem Statement

Early users frequently abandoned the app before entering their first match lobby. The team lacked a clear view of which parts of the onboarding or navigation experience were causing hesitation.

Goals & Success Metrics

  • Reduce drop-off between registration and first lobby entry.
  • Shorten time-to-first-session by simplifying onboarding steps.
  • Improve clarity of wallet/KYC expectations for new users.
  • Define consistent early funnel KPIs for future measurement.

Stakeholders & Cadence

Collaborated with the Product Manager, Tech Lead, Design Lead, and QA Lead. Cadence included weekly backlog refinement, twice-weekly standups, and monthly reviews for funnel and UX insights.

End-to-End Approach

  1. Discovery — Audited onboarding flow, reviewed common user issues, mapped the “install → first match” journey.
  2. Problem Identification — Flagged unclear wallet/KYC messaging and inconsistent match availability indicators.
  3. Requirements Clarification — Documented simplified flows and clarified CTAs with PM and devs.
  4. Design Collaboration — Supported design in creating clearer entry paths and improvement concepts.
  5. Build Support — Validated events in test builds and logged defects tied to onboarding.
  6. Review & Recommendations — Summarized friction points and aligned improvements for the next sprint cycles.

Key Findings & Recommendations

  • New players preferred a direct “Quick Match” path instead of multi-step onboarding prompts.
  • Wallet/KYC steps felt overwhelming on first open; deferring non-critical instructions reduced hesitation.
  • Room availability indicators needed to reflect real-time status to prevent user confusion.
  • Consistent terminology across onboarding and lobby screens reduced back-navigation and drop-offs.

Impact & Results

While no sensitive metrics are shared, the streamlined flows and clearer onboarding guidance enabled the team to reduce early friction, improve the perceived clarity of the first-time user path, and establish a more structured KPI framework for future decision-making.
